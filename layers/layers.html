Layers

Layer Panel

In the bottom right corner of the Photoshop window is the layer panel. Here you can see all of the layers in your project. The layer order matters. Whatever is on top will be displayed in front, and whatever's on the bottom will be displayed behind.

Reordering

You can change the order of layers by clicking and draging them. Notice how what's on top changes.

Hiding

You can hide layers by clicking the eyeball next to each layer.

Opacity

You can make images transparent by changing its opacity with this slider.

Blending Modes

Layers can blend together with the layers beneath them. There are many different blending modes to choose from to get cool effects.

Locking

Layers can be locked to prevent further editing. Locked layers can't be clicked. The layer below will be clicked instead.

Styles

Layers can have style effects applied to them, like outlines, drop shadows, and more. You can customize them in the layer style menu.

Select Multiple Layers

You can select multiple layers at the same time. Hold shift while clicking a layer to select a range, or hold CTRL to select specific layers out of order.
