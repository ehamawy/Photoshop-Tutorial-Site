Tools and Shortcuts

Toolbar

On the left side of the Photoshop window is the toolbar. Here you can select between different tools to do all sorts of things, like select, erace, draw, crop, and much more! Some tool variants are hidden under submenus. Try right clicking a tool to see its variants!

As a shortcut, you can also change tools by pressing its corresponding key on your keyboard. Memorize them all to become a Photoshop Pro!

Move

Shortcut: V

The move tool lets you move layers and objects around your artboard. Press Ctrl+T to select the bounds of your object, move and resize it however you like, and then press enter to commit your changes.

You can also hold shift to allow the image to stretch, instead of having it maintain its aspect ratio.

Rectangular Marquee

Shortcut: M

The rectangular marquee tool lets you select only a certain portion of a layer. Think of this as your regular select tool.

You can mix this with our Ctrl+T shortcut from earlier to move pixels around.

Lasso

Shortcut: L

The lasso tool lets you select only a certain portion of a layer, but instead of just rectangles, you can draw the section you want to edit instead.

Crop

Shortcut: C

The crop tool allows you to set the edges and size of your image. Click and drag the handles to crop, and when you're satisfied, press enter to commit the changes.

Brush

Shortcut: B

The brush tool allows you to draw with your mouse. There are many different brushes to choose from in the top left. It will always use the currently selected color in the top right.

Paint Bucket

Shortcut: B

The paint bucket tool "flood fills" any enclosed space with a color. Make sure the area you're filling is fully enclosed, otherwise you might fill areas you don't mean to, like the surrounding areas or background.

Text

Shortcut: T

The text tool allows you to place textboxes. This way you can have words in your images. With this tool you have to click the checkbox to commit your changes, as the enter key adds a new line instead.

There are basic options on the top bar for changing things like typeface, weight, and font size.

On the right there are also more advanced options under Character and Paragraph panels. Here you can configure kerning between characters, ligatures, and other advanced text options.
